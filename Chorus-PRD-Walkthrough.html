The discovery desert.

DAF capital is the most flexible money on earth — already given, irrevocable, free to take risk. And yet 99% of it sits in index funds, governed by the risk posture of a retirement account.

The Playground Paradox

Already given. Still hoarded.

The capital has already left the donor's estate. There is no downside to deploying it boldly — yet the surrounding infrastructure (compliance, advisors, onboarding flows) treats it like retirement savings. We replace that infrastructure with something built for the work the capital was meant to do.

02 — The Product

Discover. Match. Playback.

Three layers, one continuous loop. The investor's identity is the input, the engine, and the output — refining itself with every action.

The Intelligence Layer

An insightful mirror, not a survey.

An AI-led conversation that probes for latent values, surfaces contradictions, and adapts its vocabulary to the user's financial literacy in real time. No branching logic — only listening.

  • Beyond stated preferences. The model probes for emotional baselines and the power relationships an investor wants to shift.
  • Dual output. An evocative Investor Playbook for humans, and a structured Investment Policy Statement for advisors.
  • Always living. Every edit by the user updates the underlying vector representation.

The Engine

An ML marketplace that reads intent, not keywords.

The investor's profile becomes a high-dimensional embedding. Funds, ventures, and peer communities live in the same vector space. Match is semantic — the system understands why, not just what.

  • Swipe-to-match UX. Every interaction feeds the model. High-frequency, low-friction signal.
  • Drift detection. When actions diverge from the Playbook, Chorus surfaces the contradiction — and offers to refine.
  • Peer matching. Other humans with adjacent embeddings. Capital travels in cohorts.

The Narrative Layer

Reporting that closes the loop.

We translate complex impact data back into the investor's own language. Whether they speak in IRRs or in lived stakes, the playback meets them where their "why" lives.

  • Personal vocabulary. The same data renders three ways — for a VC, a first-time philanthropist, or a family member.
  • Non-financial proof. Power transferred. Tenants housed. Practitioners funded. Surfaced alongside IRR.
  • Quarterly rituals. Reporting that invites reflection, not just review.
